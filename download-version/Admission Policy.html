Admission Policy

  • Home
  • About Us

1. Eligibility Criteria

  • Nationality: Applicants must be Indian citizens. Overseas Citizens of India (OCI) registered under Section 7A of the Citizenship Act, 1955, are also eligible upon providing valid registration proof.
  • Age Limit: Candidates must have completed 17 years of age on or before 31st December of the year of admission.
  • Educational Qualification: Applicants must have passed the Higher Secondary Certificate (Academic) examination conducted by the Tamil Nadu State Board or CBSE, with any one of the following subject groups:
    • Physics, Chemistry, Botany, and Zoology
    • Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and any other subject

2. Nativity (Applicable only for Tamil Nadu students)

  • Applicants must be natives of Tamil Nadu.
  • Those who have studied in Tamil Nadu from Class 8 to 12 for at least 3 to 3.5 years need not submit a Nativity Certificate.
  • If required, the Nativity Certificate must be supported by a copy of the Passport, Aadhaar, Voter ID, or Driving License.
  • Submission of false nativity information will result in cancellation of admission and legal action.

3. Minimum Eligibility Marks

  • The minimum marks required for different categories (OC, BC, MBC/DNC, SC/SCA/ST) will be specified in the official prospectus for the respective academic year.

4. Application Process

  • Applicants are advised to read all instructions carefully before completing the application form.
  • The application must be filled out legibly and completely. Incomplete forms or missing enclosures will lead to rejection.
  • Unsigned applications or those lacking a recent passport-size photograph will not be accepted.
  • Scrutiny forms should not be folded, pinned, or stapled.
Important Note
  • If any incorrect information or forged document is found at any stage:
    • The admission will be forfeited.
    • The candidate may be barred from admission for two years.
    • Legal and criminal action will be initiated.
Required Enclosures (Photocopies)
  • HSC Mark Sheet
  • Transfer Certificate
  • Nativity Certificate (if applicable)
  • Permanent Community Certificate (if applicable)
  • Service Certificate (for in-service candidates with a minimum of 2 years of regular service)
  • Eligibility Certificate (for candidates from other states or universities)
  • Community Certificates from other states will not be considered.

5. Admission Procedure

  • Admissions will be processed based on merit and in accordance with the guidelines issued by the affiliating university and competent authorities.
  • Candidates are advised to retain copies of all submitted documents for future reference.
  • Admission is subject to the availability of seats and compliance with reservation policies.

6. Postgraduate Admissions (M.Pharm / M.Sc Nursing / MPT)

  • Admissions are merit-based.
  • For M.Sc Nursing and MPT programs, university exam marks and reservation norms are considered.

7. Arts and Science Admissions

  • Undergraduate Programs: Candidates must have passed 10+2 or an equivalent examination from a recognized board.
  • Postgraduate Programs: A relevant undergraduate degree from Bharathiar University or any other university recognized as equivalent by Bharathiar University is required.
Admission Process
  • Direct admission is available for select UG programs such as B.Voc (Business Process Services and Data Analysis) and B.Voc (Multimedia and Animation).
  • For PG courses, Bharathiar University conducts entrance examinations and also considers UG academic performance for admission.
Required Enclosures
  • Attested copies of the qualifying examination mark sheet or provisional mark sheet
  • Community Certificate (if applicable)
  • Certificates for special quotas (Differently Abled, Sports, NCC, Ex-Servicemen, etc.)
  • Original certificates must be produced during counseling. Failure to do so will result in cancellation of admission.

8. Rank List Preparation for UG Courses

  • Admission is based solely on marks obtained in the Higher Secondary (or equivalent) examination.
  • A rank list will be prepared accordingly.

9. Admission Procedure for PG Courses

  • Candidates must have completed a 3-year degree course (10+2+3 pattern) from a recognized university.
  • The deadline for submitting filled-in applications is 10 working days from the publication of UG results.
  • For PG programs (except MBA and MCA), only Part III marks (Major, Allied, and Ancillary subjects) will be considered.
  • Any graduate is eligible to apply for the Master of Social Work (MSW) program.
