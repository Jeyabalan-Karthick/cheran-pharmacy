1. Admission & University Requirements

  • Admission is confirmed only upon full payment of the prescribed fee and submission of relevant documents.
  • Students must obtain an eligibility certificate from the university if required.
  • Non-submission of original documents (mark sheet, migration/transfer certificate, gap certificate) may result in cancellation of admission.

2. Fee Payment Schedule

  • Course fees are determined by university norms.
  • Freshers must pay the entire fee in full. Existing students may opt for two installments as per guidelines.

3. Late Payment Fines

  • Arts & Science Students: ₹20 per day
  • Cheran Institute of Health Sciences Students: ₹100 per day

4. Hostel Fee Regulations

  • Tuition Fee Delay: ₹50 per day
  • Hostel Fee Delay: ₹50 per day
  • Timely fee payment is the sole responsibility of students and their guardians.
  • Course fees are subject to revision as per university regulations.

5. Dress Code

  • All students must follow the dress code within campus premises.
  • Paramedical Students:
    • Male: As per the prescribed paramedical uniform
    • Female: As per the prescribed paramedical uniform
  • Arts, Science & Engineering Students:
    • Male: Formal pants and shirt
    • Female: Chudidhar with neatly pinned dupatta
  • Note: Identity cards must be worn at all times.

6. Attendance Policy

  • Attendance in all theory and practical classes is mandatory.
  • Minimum Requirement:
    • Paramedical & Engineering: 80%
  • Leave must be formally requested through the faculty in charge or Principal.
  • Absence for more than 7 days must be reported to the department head.
  • Absence beyond 3 days requires a valid medical certificate.

7. Library Regulations

  • Textbooks must be returned within 7 days after exams. Late submissions attract fines as decided by the Library Committee.
  • Lost or damaged books must be compensated along with a ₹100 procurement fee.
  • Reference books are strictly for in-library use only.

8. Laboratory Regulations

  • Any damage to lab equipment, computers, or apparatus must be compensated by the student.
  • All lab activities must be performed under the supervision of assigned faculty.

9. Internal Assessment

  • Two internal exams are conducted each academic year.
  • Students failing to secure minimum qualifying marks in internal exams will be considered failed in the final university examinations.

10. Games & Sports

  • Active participation in sports and physical education is encouraged.
  • Selection for individual and team sports events is conducted by the Sports Officer.

11. Transfer Certificate (TC)

  • Students must submit a TC request signed by the Head of the Institution.
  • A No-Dues form must be submitted through the online portal (applicable for 2019 batch onwards).
  • TC will be issued after payment of the applicable fees.
